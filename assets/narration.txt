Every flight hides an invisible operation. Cargo boxes, passenger bags, ramp teams, belts, trolleys, aircraft holds and SLA clocks all move together under pressure.
But disruption rarely begins as disruption. It begins as a small signal: a box moving outside the ideal pattern, a baggage flow becoming irregular, a work zone becoming congested, or a safety step missed in motion.
This airline logistics intelligence system turns those signals into real-time operational understanding. Cameras do not merely record. They inspect.
Mathematical models detect cargo damage, score severity, timestamp evidence and connect each event to the journey of the asset.
The same intelligence protects passenger baggage, identifying damage patterns before they become arrival complaints, service failures or brand escalations.
People flow is analysed for safety, compliance and productivity. The goal is not surveillance. The goal is safer work zones, faster response and better support for teams on the ground.
Then the control tower brings everything together. Computer vision, operational data, SLA timers, incident signals, dynamic cargo pricing and Gen BI agents become one command layer.
The supervisor can ask which flight is at risk, which belt is slowing us down, which cargo item needs inspection, what caused the risk, and what action will recover the SLA.
The system simulates corrective actions before execution. It can recommend staff redeployment, baggage prioritisation, cargo re-routing, inspection lane balancing or pricing action based on live capacity and demand.
This is the shift from reporting incidents to preventing them. From fragmented dashboards to conversational operations control. From delayed reactions to proactive SLA engineering.
The result is simple: cargo moves cleaner, baggage moves safer, people work better, and airline logistics becomes predictable, intelligent and resilient.
